The craft

Restoring a slab costs a fraction of tearing it out and pouring a new one — and most concrete is a far better candidate for it than people think.

The default advice homeowners get about worn concrete is to tear it out and repour — because that’s the job most concrete companies are set up to sell. But unless a slab has genuinely failed structurally, what looks like the end of its life is usually surface damage: erosion, staining, spalling, and hairline cracking that restoration addresses directly.

Restoration means deep cleaning, repairing pits and cracks, resurfacing where the damage calls for it, then re-coloring and sealing the result. The finish is new; the slab underneath — which was never the problem — stays. Days of work instead of weeks, no demolition, no hauling, no torn-up landscaping, typically at a fraction of replacement cost.

We’re honest about the exceptions. Slabs with structural failure, severe settling, or root heave need more than a finish, and our assessment will tell you so plainly. But across coastal North County — from Del Mar courtyards to Carlsbad pool decks — the great majority of “hopeless” driveways, patios, and pool decks we evaluate are restoration candidates — and the before-and-after photos are why this is our favorite work.

What restoration can fix

  • Pitting and surface erosion — the gritty, eaten-away texture coastal exposure leaves on unsealed concrete.
  • Set-in staining — decades of oil, rust, and irrigation minerals that cleaning alone won’t lift.
  • Spalling and flaking — surface layers breaking away, often from old coatings or freeze-free coastal moisture cycling.
  • Hairline and cosmetic cracking — repaired and blended into a re-colored finish rather than left as gray scars.

Common questions

Questions we get before every job.

How do I know whether my concrete needs restoration or full replacement?

The dividing line is structural. Surface problems — pitting, staining, spalling, hairline cracks — restore well. Structural problems — large displaced cracks, significant settling or heave, slabs rocking underfoot — generally don’t. Most homeowners can’t tell the difference from above, which is exactly what the no-cost assessment is for, and we’ll give you a straight answer even when it isn’t the one that hires us.

What does restoration cost compared to replacement?

Typically a fraction — commonly somewhere between a third and a half of demolition-and-repour cost, depending on the slab’s condition and the finish chosen, and without the landscaping damage and multi-week timeline a teardown brings. Every quote is written against your actual concrete, so you’ll see the real numbers side by side before deciding.

How long does restored concrete last?

Properly restored and sealed concrete performs like finished concrete, because that’s what it now is — the underlying slab was sound all along. The finish carries the same maintenance rhythm as our other work: resealing every two to four years depending on exposure, which we can put on a schedule so you never think about it.

Can you restore concrete around pools, including the deck and rockwork?

Yes — pool surrounds are a specialty. Decks are restored and finished with our grit-infused anti-slip sealing, and faded waterslides and artificial rockwork are re-colored and re-textured by hand. It’s common for us to restore the entire pool environment in one project.
