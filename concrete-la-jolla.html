Local knowledge

La Jolla concrete lives in the hardest conditions on the coast.

Salt spray off the Cove, year-round marine layer, and intense afternoon sun put more stress on exterior concrete in La Jolla than anywhere else we work. Pool decks chalk and fade, and sealers fail early if they were ever applied at all.

Most of the concrete we see here doesn't need to be torn out. A driveway in Muirlands or a pool surround in La Jolla Shores can usually be restored, re-colored, and sealed against the salt air for a fraction of replacement cost — without weeks of demolition on the property.

We work the way estate properties expect: samples approved before full application, clean job sites, discreet crews, and finishes judged against the home itself — not against other concrete.

What La Jolla conditions do to concrete

  • Salt spray accelerates surface erosion and breaks down unsealed concrete fastest within a half mile of the water — Bird Rock, the Shores, and Windansea homes see it first.
  • Marine layer moisture keeps surfaces damp through summer mornings, feeding efflorescence (the white haze) and mildew on shaded patios.
  • UV exposure fades stained and integrally colored concrete that was never UV-rated sealed — the washed-out gray most pool decks turn.
  • Slick sealed surfaces are a real slip hazard on pool decks; standard glossy sealers get dangerous when wet. Our grit-infused system grips bare feet without feeling rough.

Common questions in La Jolla

Questions we get before every job.

How often should concrete be resealed this close to the ocean?

In La Jolla's salt-air zone we recommend inspection every two years and resealing roughly every two to three, depending on exposure and the product used. Homes west of La Jolla Boulevard sit on the aggressive end of that range. We offer scheduled resealing so it's maintained, not rescued.

Can you restore our pool deck without tearing it out?

Usually, yes. Unless the slab has structural failure, most faded or worn decks can be cleaned, repaired, re-colored, and sealed with an anti-slip finish in days — not the weeks a demolition and repour takes, and without heavy equipment on the property.

Do you work around landscaping, stonework, and finishes we can't risk?

That's the standard here and we plan for it. Adjacent surfaces are masked and protected, samples are approved before full application, and the site is left clean every day. You'll see exactly what the finish looks like before we commit to the full surface.
